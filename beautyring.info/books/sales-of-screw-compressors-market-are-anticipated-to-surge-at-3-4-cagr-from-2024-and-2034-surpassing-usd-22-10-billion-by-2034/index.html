    Industry Players Develop Novel Innovations in Screw to Navigate Evolving Industrial Landscape. Sales of screw compressors in the USA are slated to surge at a 2.7% CAGR over the assessment period. Growing demand for rotary screw compressors across various industries, along with the presence of leading market players in the country will propel the growth in the market over the forecast period

    NEWARK, Del, July 08, 2024 (GLOBE NEWSWIRE) — According to a Future Market Insights (FMI), the screw compressor market is expected to reach USD 15.82 Billion in 2024. Automation trends are driving up the demand for oil-free screw compressors. Sales of screw compressors are predicted to increase at a 3.4% CAGR, reaching USD 22.10 Billion by 2034. Screw compressors are mostly employed in high-volume, low-pressure applications with twin-shaft rotating lobe machinery because they provide dry, clean compressed air. They keep an even airflow to fulfill ongoing air demand and guarantee reliable functioning.

    The market for screw compressors is anticipated to grow significantly over the next decade, driven by the growing use of remote sensing technologies. This cutting-edge technology allows users to monitor and modify air compressor operations remotely, increasing productivity and convenience. Consequently, it is anticipated that screw compressor demand is expected to soar across several industries, including commercial, residential, and industrial applications.

    The global screw compressor market is experiencing significant growth due to rapid industrialization and widespread adoption across industries like chemical, petrochemical, metal, mining, automotive, and oil and gas. These compressors deliver premium-quality compressed air with higher efficiencies, and their use in transferring gas, flare elimination, and vapor recovery is gaining popularity. They are likely to operate under extreme weather conditions, requiring minimal maintenance and reducing operational costs.

    Which Factors are Boosting Demand for Screw Compressors?

    Oil-free systems with increased precision, efficiency, and capacity are in high demand. Furthermore, cost reductions from minimizing auxiliary equipment would propel sales of industrial air compressors. As next-generation screw compressors are eco-friendlier, and offer ease of installation and inexpensive maintenance, consumers are expected to transition from traditional equipment toward current technology. Furthermore, the growing usage of remote sensing technology, which can remotely monitor and adjust the operation of an air compressor, is fueling market expansion.

    Screw compressors are becoming adopted in the automotive, pharmaceutical, food and beverage, and oil and gas industries. Amid high demand for screw compressors, manufacturers can now mass-produce complicated items and equipment in less time. They are offering safe, low-cost, energy-efficient screw compressors to improve sales.

    Why are Sales of Screw Compressors in the Automotive Sector Rising?

    Passenger cars are one of the key contributors to air pollution, necessitating a wide spectrum of evolutionary and inventive solutions. The most commonly used fuel in such cars, gasoline (petrol), contributes to vehicular pollution by emitting carbon dioxide, nitrogen oxides, and unburned hydrocarbons.

    An automobile powered by air might be a viable alternative to a gasoline-powered vehicle. Air, which is plentiful and free of pollutants, may be compressed at a greater pressure for a minimal cost. CAVs (compressed air-powered vehicles) are environmentally friendly and provide an effective means of producing and transferring electricity. Tata Motors and Motor Development International (MDI), a French company, decided to work together on the development of a compressed-air vehicle in 2007.

    The compressor does not completely stop when the reservoir pressure reaches the necessary pressure and the automatic pressure is dropped out of the idling position to install the load/unload system in screw compressors. As a result, a smaller reservoir capacity can be employed. In the case of piston compressors with an on/off system, the reservoir’s high capacity should be employed to prevent the compressor from cycling on and off, resulting in increased power costs and the need for replacement.

    Competitive Landscape of the Screw Compressor Market:

    The industry for screw compressors is expanding rapidly due to a desire to obtain a competitive advantage and fulfill the increasing demand from different sectors. To meet new issues, manufacturers are aggressively investigating and creating technical capabilities. For example, they are refining oil-lubricated compressors to withstand blockages caused by oil. The goal of creative engineering and design is to increase oil circulation, reduce impediments, and increase the life of oil-lubricated screw compressors.

    There is also a move toward electric compressors, which aligns with sustainability objectives and environmental safety regulations. Switching from conventional oil-lubricated compressors to electric versions may cut carbon emissions, lessen reliance on fossil fuels, and improve environmental performance. Due to its advantages over other compressors, including lesser environmental impact, less maintenance requirements, and increased energy efficiency, electric screw compressors are becoming more and more appealing to consumers and environmentally aware enterprises.

    The dynamic evolution of the screw compressor industry reflects the industry’s dynamic evolution, with manufacturers expected to continue innovation and adapt to changing market demands.
